Atelier

Atelier is an open welcoming digital platform for people of all skill to learn and share DIY projects.

Topic of Interest

We were asked to design something for reducing the environmental impact of human activities.

We initially identified fast fashion but through preliminary research we discovered that the core issue in that space was the pace of consumption and waste being produced.

We decided to scope down to consumer waste, discovering the activity of Upcycling and the culture of DIY

Discovery

Consumers create waste by discarding products that they no longer need. Atelier seeks to remove that waste by helping the process of upcycling or turning used materials into higher quality products.

Upcycling

What is Upcycling

Upcycling is the reuse of discarded objects or materials in such a way as to create a product of higher quality or value than the original.

Benefits of Upcycling

For Society

Upcycling promotes a circular economy which takes materials that would usually become waste and put it back into the supply chain.

For Individuals
    Upcycling helps you
  • Recognize self-promotion within waste
  • Gain environmental awareness
  • Contribute to a circular environment
  • Become aware of products’ life cycles

Biggest Obstacles to Upcycling

Transparency

A lot of information can be hidden or not explained in tutorials causing confusion

Ability

DIY has a steep learning curve that makes it difficult for those without the necessary skills or experience to start

Discouragement

It is very easy for a person to feel discouraged and give up due to the difficulty of a project 

Materials

Many tutorials ask for specific and expensive materials and tools that the user may not have available

Sustainability is a Mindset

We found that there are connections between punk communities, sustainability communities, and DIY communities, indicating that if we can leverage a punk-like frugality to help people engage in DIY activities, we can teach people how to be sustainable in all parts of their lives.

DIY activities can shape Mindsets

Engaging in DIY activities, gives users first-hand knowledge about the processes and waste made when creating things

Makes users more eco-conscious throughout their entire lives.

Target Audience

Female Lead

While members of the DIY community generally match the demographics of the US population with regards to age, gender, and ethnicity, females younger than 55 specifically make up a large majority of the craft/upcycling niche.   

Digital

Social Media is a huge component of the DIY community. As much as 78% start their research online and 88% research the product online before buying materials. They rely mostly on Youtube and Pinterest for inspiration and use a mobile device when searching for DIY projects. 

Sustainability > Brand

As much as 80% said that they always review the materials used in products to ensure they are ethically-sources, sustainable, or upcycled before purchasing.

50% indicted they want to re-purpose products they have in home.

63% say that they don’t care about the brand of the product at all. 

Want Information

79% of DIYers use their phone in store to look up information on a project other than price.

A majority say that pricing information, reviews, and how to videos are the most helpful bits of information with DIY projects.

“Lack of information” is the top complaint DIYers have about websites.

Ideation

We found that our concepts lived on opposite ends of a spectrum. One-side being more impulse-oriented and the other being more detailed and thoughtful.

Concept Spectrum

Impulse-Oriented

Video-focused platform with short overviews first and details second

Detail-Oriented

Text-heavy deep-dives into project details and conversation in an e-commerce format

  • Comments and reviews
  • Swipe for related content
  • Creator dashboard
  • Custom DIY-focused fields
  • Creator analytics, stats, and metrics

Design

Our goal is to provide an open, welcoming space for people of all skills to find, complete, and share DIY projects.

Features

Homepage

  • Endlessly scrollable and searchable content
  • Divided into “Explore” and “Category”
  • Categories with custom user-input sub-categories

Tutorial Page

  • Side-by-side written and recorded tutorials broken into steps and phases
  • Materials, tools, and directions clearly defined
  • Public, searchable comments and threads
  • Sectioned video timeline with navigable steps
  • Sustainability-based labels backed by moderator system

Creator Tools

  • Native editing tools and DIY-specific upload fields
  • Insights to viewer stats and data

Moderator System

A video is reported for incorrect categories

An unmarked mod reviews the dispute

The creator is notified of any changes

The creator can dispute any changes to be reviewed by 2 more mods

By utilizing creator knowledge, categorization, and a double-blind moderator system to provide detailed, community-oriented tutorials.

How might we foster a community that promotes sustainability through an accessible platform that meets the users needs while making DIY a gateway to upcycling?

By providing engaging, endless content that connects creators with users on a project-specific basis.

Feedback

Problem:

Users preferred to see skill level and time on the thumbnail


Solution:

Display skill level and time rather than creator

Problem:

Users want more ways to see content related to specific creators without the social media "follow" button


Solution:

Have ways to bookmark creators and see more like a creator

Problem:

Users expect subtitles and captions


Solution:

Add a closed caption button on the video player

Problem:

Users want an onboarding process to learn the functions of the app

Solution:

Add an onboarding process

Problem:

Users want more information about the time the project will take


Solution:

Not only show overall project time, but divide it into prep, build, and wait time

Problem:

Users want to be able to see review photos posted by other users


Solution:

Add ability to upload photos to comments

Problem:

Users want to see the end product before starting a tutorial within the tutorial video player


Solution:

Add a thumbnail of the final product in the beginning of the video

Problem:

Users want an estimation of cost in the video player


Solution:

Add dollar signs to show if the project is cheap or expensive; $, $$, $$$

Problem:

Users want some sort of video rating system


Solution:

Add a rating system with face emojis to scale the satisfaction and the quality level of the video

Problem:

Users want the name of the chapter in the video playback bar


Solution:

Add chapter titles to playback bar
